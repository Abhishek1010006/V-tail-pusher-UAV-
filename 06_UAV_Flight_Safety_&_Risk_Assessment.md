# UAV Flight Safety & Risk Assessment
### Standard Operating Procedures & Safety Manual

## Document Purpose & Scope

This document establishes the comprehensive safety protocols, risk mitigations, and standard operating procedures (SOP) for the flight testing phase of the custom-developed V-Tail Pusher Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV). The primary objective is to systematically identify potential operational hazards and implement strictly structured controls to guarantee the absolute safety of all testing personnel, spectators, equipment, and surrounding property. Compliance with these procedures is mandatory for all members of the flight team during every experimental or routine flight operation.

## Aircraft Specifications

The aircraft under assessment is a specialized fixed-wing platform utilizing an efficient pusher propeller configuration and a high-stability V-tail empennage, engineered for optimal aerodynamic performance and field durability.

## Comprehensive Risk Assessment

Prior to field deployment, an evaluation of critical mission hazards has been conducted. Below is the structured risk mitigation matrix targeting physical, mechanical, environmental, and RF-based failure modes:

| Identified Hazard | Potential Consequences | Mandatory Mitigation Protocol |
|---|---|---|
| **Rotating Propeller** | Severe lacerations, deep tissue trauma, or serious bodily injury. | Personnel must never approach or cross the arc of an armed aircraft. Handlers must wear heavy-duty gloves when holding or tuning near the propulsion unit. |
| **Battery Fire (LiPo)** | Thermal runaway, catastrophic airframe loss, secondary property fires. | Conduct rigorous physical inspections of Lithium Polymer cells prior to connection. Keep a dedicated, rated fire extinguisher within immediate reach. |
| **Radio & Control Link Failure** | Uncontrolled flight, loss of situational awareness, flyaway. | Execute a thorough transmitter/receiver range check before every takeoff. Verify appropriate automated failsafe routines are active. |
| **Structural Failure** | Mid-air breakup, secondary collision risks upon rapid descent. | Perform an exhaustive pre-flight structural stress inspection of the main airframe, spar joins, and tail mounts. |
| **Servo or Motor Malfunction** | Instantaneous loss of attitude or altitude control. | Verify precise mechanical travel and electronic control responses across all surfaces prior to launch clearance. |
| **Adverse Weather / Wind** | Aero-structural overload, severe drifting, or unstable stalls. | Prohibit flight testing whenever wind speeds or local weather parameters exceed defined safe operating thresholds. |
| **Unsanctioned Spectators** | Direct risk of personal injury from collisions or landing anomalies. | Establish and strictly enforce a minimum 30-meter safety perimeter around active flight zones. |

## Flight Team Logistics & Safety Equipment

### Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

All ground and flight crew members within the active operational zone are required to utilize the following safety assets:

- Safety glasses
- Closed-toe footwear
- Heavy-duty protective gloves (mandatory when handling live propellers)
- An accessible first-aid kit
- A certified chemical fire extinguisher

### Crew Roles & Responsibilities

- **Test Leader:** Responsible for conducting the final safety briefing, evaluating field conditions, and granting official flight authorization.
- **Pilot:** Conducts system checks and maintains manual or semi-automated flight path control under safe parameters.
- **Spotter:** Maintains continuous visual line of sight with the aircraft and actively monitors airspace and boundaries for external incursions.
- **Ground Crew:** Manages physical battery handling, system initialization, and aircraft arming/disarming sequences safely clear of the propeller arc.

## Operational Checklists & Procedures

### Pre-Flight System Checklist

- [ ] Main wings securely locked/bolted
- [ ] Receiver link functional & verified
- [ ] V-tail linkages tight and secure
- [ ] Control surfaces moving in correct directions
- [ ] Propeller tight, balanced, and undamaged
- [ ] Radio range check completed successfully
- [ ] Brushless motor secure inside mount
- [ ] Center of Gravity (CG) precisely verified
- [ ] Battery at full charge voltage & fastened
- [ ] Local flight sector entirely clear

### Arming Protocol

1. Clear all personnel and equipment away from the rear pusher propeller area.
2. Loudly announce **"Arming Aircraft"** to establish field awareness.
3. Connect the primary flight battery securely into the airframe harness.
4. Allow the Electronic Speed Controller (ESC) to finish initialization and telemetry tones.
5. Check flight control surfaces for smooth, full range of movement.
6. Verify that the transmitter throttle stick is confirmed at absolute zero before enabling live power.

### Launch & Post-Flight Procedures

**Launch Execution:** Confirm prevailing wind direction directly into the nose. Ensure the immediate flight path is free of obstacles. Verbally announce takeoff to the field, apply full throttle smoothly, hand-launch the aircraft straight and level, and monitor the climb rate continuously until a safe cruising altitude is established.

**Post-Flight Sequence:** Immediately upon a successful landing, approach the aircraft from the side, disconnect the flight battery pack, and check the structural temperatures of the motor, ESC, and LiPo cells. Conduct a close visual inspection of the propeller and overall airframe structural integrity, then conclude by saving telemetry/flight log data for engineering review.

## Final Flight Clearance (Go/No-Go Criteria)

Every field deployment requires unanimous check-offs across these fundamental parameters before launch clearance is certified:

- [ ] Environmental weather acceptable
- [ ] Flight control directions correct
- [ ] Full structural airframe inspection complete
- [ ] Center of Gravity (CG) verified
- [ ] Battery health and voltage checked
- [ ] Flight area and clearing boundaries safe
- [ ] Propeller attachment secure and checked
- [ ] Emergency suppression equipment available
- [ ] Radio frequency and range test passed
